What is a physical quantity?

A physical property that can be measured and described by a number.

What are the two types of physical quantities?

1. Fundamental quantities (e.g., Mass, Length, Time, Temperature)
2. Derived quantities (e.g., Area, Speed, Volume, Force)

What is a unit of measurement?

The standard used for the measurement of a physical quantity.

What is the difference between accuracy and precision?

•Accuracy: Closeness of a measurement to the true value.
•Precision: Limit to which a physical quantity is measured.

What are significant figures?

The total number of digits (reliable digits + last uncertain digit) obtained from a measurement

What are the rules for counting significant figures?

1. All non-zero digits are significant.
2. Zeros between non-zero digits are significant.
3. Leading zeros are not significant.
4. Trailing zeros are significant if they are to the right of a decimal point.

What are exact numbers?

Numbers that are either defined or the result of a count, having an infinite number of significant figures.

What are the rules for rounding off a measurement?

1. If the digit to be dropped is less than 5, then the preceding digit is left unchanged
2. If the digit to be dropped is more than 5, then the preceding digit is raised by one
3. If the digit to be dropped is 5 followed by digits other than zero, then the preceding digit is raised by one.
4. If the digit to be dropped is 5 followed by zero or nothing, the last remaining digit is increased by 1 if it is odd, but left as it is if even.

What is an error in measurement?

The difference between the actual value of a quantity and the value obtained by a measurement.

What is absolute error?

The magnitude of the difference between the mean value and each individual value.

How are errors combined in different mathematical operations?

• Addition/Subtraction: ΔX = ΔA + ΔB
• Multiplication/Division: ΔX/X = (ΔA/A) + (ΔB/B)
• Power: ΔX/X = n(ΔA/A)

What is the standard error of the mean (SEM)?

The standard error of the mean (SEM) is expressed as σ= σ/√n, where σ is the standard deviation of the population and n is the size of the sample.
