Structure of the Atom (1909)

• Plum pudding model by J.J. Thomson
• Electrons floating in a soup of positive charge.

Rutherford's Atomic Model

• Ernest Rutherford studied alpha emission from radioactive elements
• Discovered that atoms have a small, dense nucleus
• Drawback: Classical theory predicts unstable atoms due to radiating electrons

Development Leading to Bohr's Model

• Dual character of electromagnetic radiation
• Quantized electronic energy levels in atoms

Wave Nature of Electromagnetic Radiation

• John Maxwell (1870) explained interaction between charged bodies and electromagnetic fields
• Electromagnetic waves or radiation produced by accelerating charged particles

Black Body Radiation

• Ideal body emitting and absorbing all frequencies
• Planck's Quantum Theory: Energy emitted or absorbed in discrete quantities (quanta)
• Energy of a quantum: E=hν

Photoelectric Effect

• Emission of electrons from a metal surface when struck by light
• Energy of ejected electrons depends on frequency, not intensity
• hν=hν∘+½mv² (m:mass of electron)

Hydrogen Emission Spectrum

• Characteristic line spectra for each element
• Used in chemical analysis to identify unknown atoms
• Rydberg formula: 1/λ = 109,677[(1/n1²)-(1/n2²)]cm⁻¹

Bohr Model of the Hydrogen Atom

• Stationary states with well-defined energies
• Angular momentum is quantized: L=nℏ
• Energy levels: En = −E∘/n²

Wave Nature of Particles

• Louis de Broglie: Particles have wave-like properties
• de Broglie's equation: λ = h/mv

Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle

• Fundamental limitation on knowing both position and momentum precisely
• Δx⋅Δp≥ℏ/2

Schrödinger Equation

• Central equation in Quantum Mechanics
• Ĥψ=Eψ
• Hamiltonian Operator Ĥ, Total Energy E

Wavefunctions

• Describe spatial distribution of a particle
• Born Interpretation: ∣ψ(x)∣² is proportional to the probability of finding the particle at

Particle in a Box Model

• Particle of mass m in 1-D box of length L
• Allowed energies:En=n²h²/8mL²
• Quantum numbers n determine energy levels
