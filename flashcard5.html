What is Molecular Dynamics?
Molecular Dynamics is a computer simulation of physical movements of atoms and molecules. The trajectories of molecules and atoms are determined by numerically solving the classical equations of motion (Newton's equations) for a system of interacting particles, where forces between the particles and potential energy are defined by molecular mechanics force fields.
When was Molecular Dynamics first accomplished?
Molecular Dynamics was first accomplished in 1957 and 1959 for a system of hard spheres by Adler.
What is the primary application of Molecular Dynamics today?
Molecular Dynamics is applied mostly in materials science and modeling of biomolecules.
What is the motivation behind Molecular Dynamics simulations?
The motivation is to conduct a computer experiment where a model is provided by theorists, but the calculations are carried out by the machine following an algorithm. This allows for the introduction of complexity and the investigation of more realistic systems, leading to a better understanding of real experiments.
What are the steps involved in Molecular Dynamics simulations?
1. Build a realistic atomistic model of the system.
2. Simulate the behavior of the system over time using specific conditions (temperature, pressure, volume, etc.).
What is the equation of motion used in Molecular Dynamics?
The equation of motion used is Newton's second law, F = ma, where F is the force exerted on the particle, m is its mass, and a is its acceleration.
How are forces calculated in Molecular Dynamics?
Forces are calculated using the gradient of the potential energy function. The force on each particle is given by F_i = -∇_i V.
What are some simple interatomic potentials used in Molecular Dynamics?
1. Lennard-Jones Potential: u^ij(r) = 4 ε [(σ/r)^12 - (σ/r)^6]
2. Born-Mayer (Tosi-Fumi) Potential: U(r_ij) = (q_i q_j / r_ij) + A_ij exp(-r_ij / ρ_ij) - (C_ij / r_ij^6)
What is the purpose of periodic boundary conditions in Molecular Dynamics?
Periodic boundary conditions are used to nullify surface effects and study the bulk properties of the system with a small number of particles. They involve replicating the simulation box to form an infinite lattice.
What is the minimum image convention in Molecular Dynamics?
The minimum image convention is adopted to prevent particles from interacting with their own images during the simulation. It ensures that interactions between atoms separated by a chosen cut-off distance or larger are neglected.
What is the radial distribution function (RDF) in Molecular Dynamics?
The radial distribution function is the probability of finding a pair of atoms a distance r apart relative to the probability for a completely uniform distribution. It indicates if there is local structure in the fluid.
What are some shortcomings of Molecular Dynamics?
1. Quality of the force field.
2. Size and time limitations.
3. Conformational freedom of the molecule.
4. Applicability only to parameterized systems.
5. Inability to simulate chemical reactions.
