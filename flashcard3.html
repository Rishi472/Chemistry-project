What is thermodynamics?

Thermodynamics is the science that deals with the relationships between heat and other forms of energy. It originated during the Industrial Revolution to maximize the efficiency of heat engines.

What is a thermodynamic system?

A thermodynamic system is any part of the universe under consideration. It can range from something simple like a beaker of water to something complex like an entire galaxy.

What are thermodynamic surroundings?

Thermodynamic surroundings are everything other than the thermodynamic system, i.e., the rest of the universe.

What are the three types of thermodynamic systems?

1. Open System: Exchanges both energy and matter with its surroundings.
2. Closed System: Exchanges energy but not matter with its surroundings.
3. Isolated System: Exchanges neither energy nor matter with its surroundings.

What is the difference between diathermic and adiabatic systems?

• Diathermic System: Allows heat flow into or out of the system.
• Adiabatic System: Prevents heat flow into or out of the system.

What are state functions?

State functions describe the state of a system. Examples include pressure (P), volume (V), temperature (T), mass (m), quantity (n), internal energy (U), enthalpy (H), entropy (S), and Gibbs energy (G).

What are path functions?

path functions govern the transition between states and describe how energy enters or leaves the system. Examples include heat (q) and work (w)

What are the four laws of thermodynamics?

Zeroth Law: If two systems are each in thermal equilibrium with a third, they are in thermal equilibrium with each other.
First Law: Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed.
Second Law: The total entropy of an isolated system can never decrease over time.
Third Law: The entropy of a system approaches a constant value as the temperature approaches absolute zero.

What is enthalpy (H)?

Enthalpy is a state function defined as H=U+pV, where U is internal energy, p is pressure, and V is volume. It is useful for describing processes at constant pressure.

What is entropy (S)?

Entropy is a state function that measures the disorder or randomness of a system. It is defined as ΔS=q(rev)/T where q(rev)is the heat exchanged reversibly and T is the temperature.

What is Gibbs energy (G)?

Gibbs energy is a state function defined as G=H−TS, where H is enthalpy,T is temperature, and S is entropy. It is used to determine the spontaneity of a process.

What is Hess's Law?

Hess's Law states that the enthalpy change of a reaction is independent of the pathway between the initial and final states. It is applicable to all state functions, including enthalpy (H), internal energy (U), entropy (S), and Gibbs energy (G)
